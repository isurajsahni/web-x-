Growth had outpaced the tools. Critical work lived in scattered spreadsheets, inboxes and apps that didn’t talk to each other. We designed and engineered a custom ERP that pulls HR, projects, finance and tenders into one place — so the team stops chasing data and starts acting on it.

The problem

Off-the-shelf ERPs are built for a generic company that doesn’t exist. They force your team to bend their process around the software’s assumptions — and you still pay per seat, forever. GPSFDK had outgrown spreadsheets but didn’t want to inherit someone else’s workflow. They needed a system shaped around their operation, not the other way round.

What we built

One dashboard, four domains

We unified the parts of the business that had drifted into separate tools — HR, projects, finance and tenders — behind a single, role-aware dashboard. Each team sees what matters to them; leadership sees the whole picture without stitching reports together by hand.

Designed for daily use

Internal software fails when it’s a chore to use, so we treated UX with the same care as a customer-facing product. Clear navigation, fast pages, sensible defaults and forms that don’t fight the user — the system disappears so the work can happen.

Built to scale

It’s a real web application, engineered to grow: new modules, more users and deeper automation can be added without a rebuild. Because it’s custom, GPSFDK owns it outright — no per-seat licence climbing as the team expands.

The best business software isn’t the one with the most features. It’s the one your team actually opens every morning.

Part of a bigger system

ERP One is the operational engine behind the GPSFDK storefront — the same brand, the same standard of craft, applied to the tools that run the business rather than the shopfront that sells it.
