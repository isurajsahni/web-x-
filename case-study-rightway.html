When you’re selling something people put in their bodies, credibility isn’t a nice-to-have - it’s the whole sale. We built Rightway a presence that looks established and feels safe, then engineered it to load fast and guide every visitor toward a single clear action: get in touch.

The brief

Rightway needed to compete with bigger, better-known purifier brands. In a category built on health and trust, a dated or slow website doesn’t just look bad - it quietly makes buyers wonder whether the product is any good either. The site had to do the heavy lifting a salesperson would: look credible, answer the obvious questions, and make the next step obvious.

Our approach

A brand that signals trust

Clean, confident, clinical-but-warm. We leaned into the visual cues people associate with safe water and reliable engineering, so the brand reads as trustworthy before a word is read.

A site built to convert

Every page is structured around one job: turn interest into an enquiry. Clear product information, prominent contact paths and a layout that removes friction rather than adding decoration - this is the conversion-first thinking we bring to any commercial site.

Fast and findable

Optimised images, lean code and clean semantic markup mean the site loads quickly even on mobile data - exactly the Core Web Vitals discipline that keeps visitors from bouncing and helps the site surface in local search for people actively looking to buy.

For a trust-led product, the website is the first handshake. It has to feel solid before it can sell.

Mobile-first, because buyers are

Most people researching a purifier do it on their phone. The whole experience was designed mobile-first - tap targets, readable type and fast loads on a small screen - so the majority of visitors get the best version of the site, not a cramped afterthought.
